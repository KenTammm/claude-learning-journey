## Section B: AI Usage Guidelines for the Team

*These guidelines apply to everyone using AI tools for company marketing and sales work.*

---

### 1. What You CAN Use AI For ✅

| Task | Notes |
|------|-------|
| Writing first drafts of copy, emails, posts | Always review before sending |
| Brainstorming campaign ideas | Use as starting point, not final answer |
| Analysing data you paste in | Verify numbers independently |
| Reformatting or restructuring content | Check output matches original intent |
| Generating multiple versions of a message | You choose the best one |
| Summarising long documents | Check summary is accurate |

---

### 2. What You CANNOT Share with AI ❌

| Do NOT share | Why |
|-------------|-----|
| Real client names + contact details | Privacy — client data stays internal |
| Internal pricing documents | Confidential business information |
| Financial reports or forecasts | Sensitive company data |
| Unreleased product information | IP protection |
| Private client correspondence | Confidentiality |

**Rule of thumb:** If you wouldn't post it publicly, don't paste it into AI.

---

### 3. Always Review Before Using ⚠️

AI output is a first draft, not a final product. Before using anything AI generates:

- [ ] Is the information accurate? (Especially statistics, claims, dates)
- [ ] Does it sound like us — our brand voice, our tone?
- [ ] Is it appropriate for this specific client or audience?
- [ ] Would you be comfortable if this was attributed to you personally?

**If the answer to any of these is NO — revise it.**

---

### 4. Be Honest About AI's Role

**With your team:**
If you use AI to draft something, say so. This helps everyone learn what works and improves our collective use of these tools.

**With clients:**
For standard marketing copy and follow-up emails, disclosure is not required. However, if a client directly asks whether AI was involved in creating a document or proposal, be honest.

**In formal documents:**
Any report, proposal, or official document that will be sent to clients or stakeholders should include a note if AI was substantially involved in its creation.

---

### 5. Our Brand Voice — What AI Gets Wrong

AI tends to produce generic, safe copy. Watch for these common errors and correct them:

| AI tends to write | We actually sound like |
|------------------|----------------------|
| "We are excited to offer..." | Direct, no hype |
| Long paragraphs explaining everything | Concise, trust the reader |
| Feature-focused ("high quality design") | Outcome-focused ("lower maintenance = more profit") |
| Overly formal B2B language | Professional but conversational |
| Generic calls-to-action ("Contact us today") | Specific, low-pressure next steps |

**Fix:** Always provide 2–3 examples of our actual copy when prompting AI for new content. This is called few-shot prompting — it dramatically improves brand voice accuracy.

If you're unsure whether something is appropriate to share with AI, or whether an AI output is good enough to use — ask Ken before proceeding.

Better to ask once than to fix a mistake after it's been sent to a client.
