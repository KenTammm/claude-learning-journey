# AI-Assisted Marketing Playbook
## Part 3: Sales Follow-Up Templates

> **Company:** Luxury Glamping Tent Solutions Provider  
> **Author:** Ken Tam | Marketing Officer, Sydney  
> **Last updated:** April 2026

---

## Overview

This section covers four real follow-up scenarios our Sales team faces. Each scenario includes:
- A ready-to-use AI prompt to generate personalised messages
- A sample output
- Channel guidance (Email / WhatsApp / LinkedIn)

**Key principle:** Every follow-up should reference something specific about the client — their property, their situation, or their conversation with us. Generic follow-ups get ignored.

---

## Scenario 1: Client Went Silent After Receiving a Quote

**When to use:** 1–2 weeks after sending a quote with no response.

**Channel:** Email or WhatsApp (depending on how they prefer to communicate)

**AI Prompt:**
> Write a follow-up message to a [client type — resort owner / farm owner / developer] who received a quote from us 1–2 weeks ago and hasn't responded.
>
> Client context: [Add what you know — their property type, location, how many tents they were considering, any specific concerns they raised]
>
> Tone: Warm, not pushy. Acknowledge they're busy. Re-open the conversation by offering value, not pressure. Under 100 words for WhatsApp, under 150 words for email.
>
> Do NOT use: "Just checking in", "Following up on my previous email", "I wanted to touch base"

**Sample Output — WhatsApp version:**
---
Hi [Name], hope the week's been good.

I know quotes can sit in the "think about it" pile for a while — totally understandable.

If anything came up after you looked it over — questions about the build process, timeline, or whether [their property type] would be the right fit — happy to jump on a quick call and talk it through.

No rush at all. Just here if it's useful.

[Your name]
---

**Sample Output — Email version:**

**Subject:** Re: Your glamping quote — any questions?

---
Hi [Name],

I wanted to follow up on the quote we sent over — not to rush you, but in case anything came up that we could help clarify.

A few things clients often want to talk through at this stage:
- Whether the design works for their specific land layout
- How the builder relationship works in practice
- Realistic timeline from order to first guests

If any of those are on your mind, happy to set up a 15-minute call. Or if the timing just isn't right yet, no problem at all — I'll leave it with you.

[Your name]

## Scenario 2: Exhibition / Event Lead Follow-Up

**When to use:** Within 48 hours of meeting a potential client at a trade show, industry event, or networking function.

**Channel:** Email or LinkedIn (more professional than WhatsApp at this early stage)

**Your process:** You collect background info on each lead and match them to the right salesperson — or handle it yourself. This template is for the first personalised touchpoint after the event.

**AI Prompt:**
> Write a follow-up message to someone I met at [event name] who is interested in glamping for their [property type / project].
>
> What I know about them:
> - Name: [Name]
> - Their situation: [what they told you — e.g. "owns a 50-acre farm in regional Victoria, wants to diversify income"]
> - Their key interest: [e.g. "wanted to understand startup costs and council approvals"]
> - Any specific thing we discussed: [e.g. "I mentioned our material estimation process"]
>
> Tone: Personal, warm, references our actual conversation. Under 150 words. Ends with a specific next step (call, site visit, or sending relevant case study).

**Sample Output:**
---
Hi [Name],

Really enjoyed our chat at [event] — it's not every day you meet someone with [specific detail about their property or vision].

You mentioned [specific thing they said — e.g. "the council approval process was your main concern"]. That's actually one of the most common questions we get, and it's very manageable with the right guidance — happy to walk you through how other clients in similar situations have handled it.

I've also pulled together a case study from a [similar property type] project that I think you'd find useful.

Would a 20-minute call this week or next work? Happy to work around your schedule.

[Your name]

## Scenario 3: Website Enquiry Follow-Up

**When to use:** Someone has submitted a contact form or sent a WhatsApp/email enquiry through the website.

**Channel:** WhatsApp (if they messaged via WhatsApp) or Email (if via contact form) — reply on the same channel they used.

**AI Prompt:**
> Write a reply to a website enquiry from someone interested in our luxury glamping tent solutions.
>
> Their message said: [paste their actual enquiry here]
>
> Tone: Warm, responsive, helpful. Answer their specific question directly — don't ignore what they asked and pivot to a sales pitch. Under 150 words. End with one clear next step.
>
> If their enquiry is vague (e.g. "I'm interested in glamping tents"), ask 2–3 qualifying questions to understand their situation before pitching.

**Sample Output — for a vague enquiry:**
---
Hi [Name], thanks for reaching out!

Luxury glamping is such an exciting space — happy to help you explore whether it's the right fit for your project.

To point you in the right direction, it'd help to know a little more:

1. What type of property are you working with? (Resort, farm, vacant land, something else?)
2. Roughly how many tents are you thinking about to start?
3. Are you based in Australia, or overseas?

No wrong answers — just helps me give you something actually useful rather than a generic brochure.

[Your name]

## Scenario 4: Re-engaging a Past Client

**When to use:** A previous client you haven't spoken to in 6+ months.

**Channel:** Start with a text/WhatsApp to warm them up, then follow up with a call. Prepare questions before calling.

**Your process:** You prepare a question list before calling past clients — this template helps you do that with AI assistance.

**Step 1 — AI Prompt to prepare your call questions:**
> I'm about to re-engage a past glamping client I haven't spoken to in [time period].
>
> What I know about them:
> - They ordered: [product type, number of tents, when]
> - Their property: [type and location]
> - Last conversation: [what you remember — any issues, plans they mentioned]
>
> Help me prepare:
> 1. A short WhatsApp message to warm them up before I call
> 2. 5 questions to ask during the call to understand their current situation and whether there's an opportunity to help them further

**Sample Output — WhatsApp warm-up:**
---
Hi [Name]! Ken here from [Company].

Hope everything's going well with the [property name / glamping setup] — it's been a while since we caught up.

I've been thinking about some of our early clients lately and wanted to check in and see how things are tracking. Any chance you'd be free for a quick call this week?

[Your name]
---

**Sample Output — Call preparation questions:**
1. How has the glamping operation been going since you opened? What's worked better than expected?
2. Have there been any challenges with the tents or setup that we should know about?
3. Are you at the capacity you planned, or are you thinking about expanding?
4. Has the market response matched what you expected when you started?
5. Is there anything you'd do differently if you were starting again — anything we could factor in if you ever wanted to add more tents?

## Master AI Prompt for Any Follow-Up Scenario

When none of the above templates fit exactly, use this:

> "Write a [WhatsApp message / email / LinkedIn message] to follow up with [client type] who [their situation].
>
> What I know about them: [paste relevant details]
> My goal for this message: [re-open conversation / book a call / send a resource / check in]
> Tone: [warm / professional / casual]
> Length: [under 100 words for WhatsApp / under 150 words for email]
>
> Important: Reference something specific about their situation. Do NOT use generic opening lines like 'just checking in' or 'hope this finds you well'."

---

## Discernment Checklist for Follow-Up Messages

Before sending any AI-generated follow-up:

- [ ] Does it reference something specific about this client? (Not generic)
- [ ] Does it avoid pushy or salesy language?
- [ ] Is the channel right? (WhatsApp = casual/short, Email = more detail, LinkedIn = professional)
- [ ] Does it have ONE clear next step — not three options?
- [ ] Does it sound like you — not like a robot?
- [ ] Would you be comfortable receiving this message yourself?
