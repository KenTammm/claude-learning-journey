# AI-Assisted Marketing Playbook
## Part 1: B2B Audience Profiles

> **Company:** Luxury Glamping Tent Solutions Provider  
> **Author:** Ken Tam | Marketing Officer, Sydney  
> **Last updated:** April 2026

---

## Overview

Before using AI to write any copy, email, or report, always refer to this document. AI needs this context to produce relevant, targeted content — without it, output will be generic and ineffective.

**Golden rule:** Paste the relevant audience profile into every AI prompt before asking it to write anything.

---

## Our Four Client Types

---

### Client Type 1: Resort Owners

**Who they are:**
Existing hospitality businesses — boutique hotels, eco-lodges, coastal resorts — looking to expand their accommodation offering without major construction.

**Their situation:**
- Already have land and infrastructure
- Want to increase revenue per guest or attract new market segments
- Have experience running accommodation businesses

**What they care most about:**
- Revenue uplift — how much more can they charge per night?
- How glamping fits alongside (not disrupts) their existing operations
- Speed to market — how quickly can they start earning?

**Their biggest fears:**
- High ongoing maintenance costs eating into profit
- Legal and compliance issues with temporary structures
- Building something that sits empty — no market demand

**The message that works:**
> "Glamping adds a premium revenue stream to your existing property without disrupting what's already working. Our clients typically charge 30–50% more per glamping night than standard rooms — with lower build costs than permanent structures."

**Proof point that moves them:**
- ROI timeline (how many months to break even?)
- Maintenance cost comparison vs permanent builds
- Occupancy data from existing clients

---

### Client Type 2: Farm & Agricultural Land Owners

**Who they are:**
Farmers or rural landowners with scenic or unique natural settings, looking to diversify income beyond traditional agriculture.

**Their situation:**
- Land is an asset that's currently underutilised for revenue
- May have limited hospitality experience
- Often family-run operations

**What they care most about:**
- Creating a new income stream from land they already own
- Simplicity — they don't want a complicated business to manage
- Whether their land is suitable

**Their biggest fears:**
- Not knowing where to start (permits, builders, setup)
- Whether tourists will actually come to their location
- Taking on too much risk with limited capital

**The message that works:**
> "You already have the most valuable asset — the land. We handle the design and planning, and guide you through the whole process, including working with local builders. Many of our farm clients started with just 3–4 tents and grew from there."

**Proof point that moves them:**
- Step-by-step process overview (so it feels manageable)
- Small-scale starting options
- Case studies from similar farm/rural properties

---

### Client Type 3: Land Developers & Tourism Operators

**Who they are:**
Investors or developers looking to activate land for tourism purposes, often with multiple sites or larger-scale ambitions.

**Their situation:**
- Think in terms of projects and returns, not lifestyle
- Often evaluating multiple investment options simultaneously
- Want scalable solutions, not one-offs

**What they care most about:**
- Total project cost vs projected revenue
- Scalability — can this be replicated across multiple sites?
- Timeline from investment to income

**Their biggest fears:**
- Legal compliance across different councils/regions
- Project blowing out in cost or timeline
- Overbuilding for the market

**The message that works:**
> "Our design system is built for scale. We've supplied projects from 4 tents to 40+, and our material estimation process ensures you know your costs before you commit. We also support compliance guidance across Australian jurisdictions."

**Proof point that moves them:**
- Portfolio of completed projects at scale
- Cost-per-tent breakdown
- Timeline from order to operational

---

### Client Type 4: Repeat Customers (Global)

**Who they are:**
Previous clients — from any of the above categories — who have returned for additional orders. We have repeat clients across Asia, Mexico, Europe, and South Asia, making this a truly international client base.

**Why this matters for marketing:**
Global repeat customers are our strongest social proof. They signal that our product works across different climates, regulations, and markets — not just Australia. This should be highlighted in all international outreach.

**Their situation:**
- Already trust our product and process
- Expanding an existing glamping operation
- May be recommending us to their network
- Located across multiple time zones and regulatory environments

**What they care most about:**
- New product updates or improvements since their last order
- Consistency — same quality they received before
- Preferential treatment as returning clients

**Their biggest fears:**
- Quality has dropped since their last order
- Process has changed in ways that complicate reordering
- Being treated like a new customer

**Regional considerations:**

| Region | Additional concerns | Message angle |
|--------|-------------------|---------------|
| 🌏 Asia | Shipping logistics, installation support | "We've delivered across Asia — here's how we handle logistics end-to-end" |
| 🌎 Mexico / Latin America | Local building regulations, tropical climate durability | "Our tents are built for heat and humidity — and we guide you through local compliance" |
| 🌍 Europe | Sustainability credentials, design standards | "Our materials meet European durability standards, with a design-led approach" |
| 🌏 South Asia | High heat and humidity resistance, local builder support | "Engineered for tropical climates, with guidance for working with local construction teams" |

**The message that works:**
> "Welcome back. Here's what's new since your last order — and here's how we make reordering straightforward for existing clients."

**For international repeat clients specifically:**
> "We've supplied glamping projects across Asia, Europe, Mexico, and South Asia. Whatever your location, our design system adapts — and we'll guide you through local requirements every step of the way."

**Proof point that moves them:**
- Product updates or new tent models
- Streamlined reorder process
- Loyalty recognition (personal touch from sales team)
- International project portfolio showing successful deliveries in their region

## Universal Pain Points (All Client Types)

These fears appear across ALL four client types. Always address at least one in every piece of content:

| Fear | How to address it |
|------|------------------|
| High maintenance costs | Emphasise commercial-grade materials, long lifespan, low upkeep |
| Legal/compliance concerns | Mention compliance guidance and council support as part of our service |
| "Will it be too expensive?" | Lead with ROI and payback period, not price |
| "Will there be demand?" | Provide market data + case studies from similar locations |

---

## Universal Desire (All Client Types)

**Every client ultimately wants one thing: more income from their land or property.**

All messaging should connect back to this — whether through:
- Higher nightly rates
- New revenue streams
- Lower operating costs = more profit
- Attracting higher-spending guests

---

## How to Use This Document with AI

When asking AI to write copy, emails, or social posts, always start your prompt with:

**Template:**
> "I'm writing [content type] for [Client Type]. Here is their profile:
> - They are: [description]
> - They care most about: [key motivation]
> - Their biggest fear is: [key objection]
> - The message that works: [key message]
> Please write [specific output] that speaks directly to their situation."

This ensures AI output is targeted, not generic.
